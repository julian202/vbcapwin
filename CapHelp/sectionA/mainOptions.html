Main Options

These options are used to select the reports and graphs to be created. Click the box next to the graph name to select it. The first eight lines contain reports and text available for Capillary Flow Porometry reports. The Pressure versus Flow Rate option is also used to display data from an Integrity Test.

Porometry Text Report
Provides a report of the Porometry test using text only.

Porometry Summary Sheet
provides a summary sheet of various test results.

Flow Rate vs. Pressure
Graphs the wet and dry flow curves as they were shown on the monitor during the actual test. A line is also plotted showing the half dry flow rate. The intersection of this line and the wet curve is the mean flow pressure.

Cum. Filter Flow vs. Diameter
The cumulative filter flow versus diameter graph shows the accumulated percentage of flow through the sample at specific diameters. The Cumulative filter flow is the percent of wet flow to dry flow and is found by adding together the different flow quantities taken at each point.

Pore Size Distribution (Histogram):
Displays the pore size distribution in the form of a histogram.

Pore Size Distribution vs. Diameter:
The pore size distribution versus diameter graph shows the concentration of pores based on average pore diameter.

Pore Size Distribution and Cumulative Filter Flow
Displays both a Cumulative Filter Flow vs. Diameter and a Pore Size Distribution on the same graph, allowing the data to be easily compared.

Frequency Distribution and Cumulative Filter Flow
Displays the frequency distribution and cumulative filter flow on the same graph.

Frequency Distribution (Log Scale)
Graphs the frequency distribution using a log scale.

Cumulative and Differential Filter Flow
Graphs both types of filter flow calculations together.

Permeability Text Report
Provides a report of the Permeability test using text only.

Permeability Graph:
Graph of pressure versus flow rate of the wet- or dry-flow curves that represent the data points taken during the test.

Darcy:
uses the Darcy formula (page 145) to calculate the Dry Curve; takes into account the specific factors of the test being performed.

Gurley:
unit of permeability that shows the amount of time it takes a specific volume of gas to pass through a sample of a specific diameter.

Frazier:
Standard measure of air permeability.

Rayl:
measures air flow resistance

Frazier Analysis
Analyzes the data to determine the Frazier value for the test material.
