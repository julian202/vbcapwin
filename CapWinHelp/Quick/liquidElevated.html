Liquid Permeability - Elevated Pressure Test

Back to Quick Start Sheet TOC  Integrity Test
  1. Prepare sample to be tested. Sample may need to be cut in order to fit and lie flat in the sample chamber. The sample should be cut bigger than the o-ring in order to completely cover the o-ring and ensure proper sealing. Samples smaller than the o-ring will require adapter plates.

  2. Disconnect any hose attached to the sample chamber lid. Unscrew and remove the sample chamber lid. Take out the spacing insert and adapter plates (if present). Check the o-rings for dryness, apply vacuum grease if needed. Make sure components are clean and free from wetting fluid left by previous testing.

  3. Under Group on the main Capwin menu, choose an existing group by clicking on Select Group Ctrl+U or make a new group by clicking on New Group Ctrl+N. A group contains the pressure units used and the Autotest setup for the last test ran using this group.

  4. Under Execute on the main Capwin menu, select Autotest F2. The Autotest settings screen will open.

  5. If the Test Type field is visible but does not show Liquid Permeability - Elevated Pressure , click on Test Type to bring up the Test Selection box.Select Liquid Permeability - Elevated Pressure . Select Liquid Permeametry. Under Liquid Permeametry Options, choose the Elevated Pressure Test.
    Click OK to return to the Autotest screen.

  6. At the Autotest screen, enter information in the designated fields by clicking on the field:
    Note: Depending on which fields the supervisor has selected to be shown, not all fields may appear in Autotest.
    Output File Name - user designated
    End User - user designated
    Test Reference - user designated, eg: Gas Permeability
    Sample ID - user designated
    Lot Number - user designated
    Operator - user designated, eg: the initials of person running test
    Fluid - enter the name of the fluid being used
    Surface Tension - enter the surface tension of the fluid being used. (dynes/cm)
    Diameter - diameter at which the sample is being tested, which is the inner diameter of the o-ring.
    Cylindrical Length - only required for samples tested in the radial direction
    Thickness - thickness of sample.


  7. If the liquid permeability test is being performed using a liquid other than water and the Liquid field is visible, click on the Liquid field to specify the liquid. This will bring up the Liquid Select dialogue box SelectX: Other Liquid and enter the name of the liquid and its surface tension in centipoise.
    Click Done to return to the Autotest screen.

  8. Place the sample into the sample chamber, making sure sample seals against o-ring.
    If adapter plates are used, place the screened adapter plate in the bottom of the sample chamber. Align the plate on the three chamber pins. Yellow chip pullers are provided to aid in putting in and taking out plates from the chamber. Place the sample on top of the screened plate. Make sure the o-ring of this screened plate seals against the sample. Place the top adapter plate into the chamber.

  9. Place the chamber insert into the chamber. The insert should sit no lower than the height of the sample chamber.

  10. Click on the Start Test button ALT+S. The Autotest sequence will now begin. A series of dialogue boxes will appear, instructing user to: Enter starting pressure, maximum pressure, point step pressure, maximum wait between points, maximum number of points. Click Continue after each entered value.

  11. A dialogue box will appear, instructing user to Load sample. Fill up the sample chamber with fluid. Make sure no air bubbles are trapped in the chamber. Screw on the lid and hand tighten. Attach the liquid fill hose with quick connect fitting to the sample chamber lid.

  12. Click OK. This will bring up a dialogue box, prompting user to Begin filling penetrometer.

  13. Click the Prefilled button if penetrometer tube has already been filled completely.
    Otherwise, locate the Liquid Fill valve. It is a black knob located on the front of the shroud of the porometer. Turn the knob to the Open position and begin filling up penetrometer tube until the counts on the screen are between 2000 and 800 counts. If the penetrometer is overfilled, it will show a count value of 1. Drain off the excess liquid by pressing on the white valve in the quick connect fitting.

  14. Click OK when the counts are between 2000 and 800 counts. A second dialogue box will open, asking "Is the penetrometer full?" Use this time to recheck once more before proceeding. Click OK when ready.

  15. The Current Test Status screen will open. Click OK to begin the test.

  16. The height of the liquid, time, pressure and flow rate are displayed as the data is collected during the test.

  17. In the preferences menu under the liquid permeability tab, there is a selection to refill the penetrometer. If the penetrometer tube has emptied completely before reaching the set maximum pressure for the test, a dialogue box will appear, prompting user to "Fill until counts are between 2000 and 800". Refill the penetrometer once more to continue with test or click on the Stop Test button to end the test at this point.

  18. When the test has ended, a dialogue box appears, instructing user to "Disconnect the penetrometer from the sample chamber and open the sample chamber to allow the fluid to drain out". Click OK.

  19. The Test Done dialogue box will then come up. Click OK.

  20. Test results may be viewed and analyzed using CapRep. To access CapRep from the Capwin main menu, select Report and click on Execute Report F4.
