Thin Samples

For very thin samples where stretching and tearing is a problem, the following method of mounting is recommended.

The sample is placed in the bottom of the sample chamber. It is covered by a thin screen that has pores larger than those in the sample. One of the inserts used to test small-diameter samples is inverted and placed on top of the screen without its O rings. The large spacing insert is then placed in the chamber sealing against the thin insert, as is normally done.

The only restriction on the sample's support is that it should have pores larger than those in the sample so that the support does NOT cause any flow restriction which could result in erroneous data.
